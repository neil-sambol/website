My Story...

It all started when I was 6 and took my father's clock-radio apart. This was not a particularly instructive effort, but I did receive an erector set for my next birthday. By my teenage years, I discovered the magic of computers and using bits and bytes to tell a machine what to do.


After an undergraduate degree in computer science and ten years as a C/C++ developer, I discovered that people were just as interesting as technology and that the combination of the two was the sweet-spot in which I wanted to spend the rest of my career.


This has been proven true through numerous successful management and leadership roles. I still find the challenges of the combination of people and technology intriguing and compelling.


I continue to look for ways in which I help technology companies disrupt the status quo and improve efficiency, reduce time to market, increase quality, and reduce the cost burdens of both the supplier and the consumer of the technologies.

Specialities

I do a lot of things well, but these I do best!

  • Hiring

    The foundation of any technology company is its engineers. Hire motivated, flexible, collaborative, problem-solvers and you and your organization will be rewarded many times over.

  • Growing Teams

    Once you hire amazing folks, it is important to guide their career growth and support them educationally. Remove roadblocks, politics, and bureaucracy from the team and open the throttle. A thoroughbred is meant to run; so is your team.

  • Agile Transformation

    Rapid iterations and incremental deliveries are the way to hit market windows and keep customers happy. Reducing sample size, tuning process, increasing automation, and removing obstacles are the way to deliver more quickly and keep your team happy as a bonus.

  • CICD Pipeline

    Getting high-quality product to market rapidly is the Holy Grail of technology. Infrastructure as code / Test Driven Development (TDD) / Continuous delivery are inexpensive and repeatable ways to get there. Let an experienced professional show you the way.

  • Mobile

    Want to stay in touch with your customers 24x7? Consider mobile applications. The truly personal computer in everyone's pocket is a way to keep in touch with customers, deliver targeted content, and provide more opportunities for service and product upgrades.

  • Slaying the monolith & scaling horizontally

    No longer able to scale vertically? Scale horizontally with microservices which also help to deploy independent functionality without risk of breaking other features.
